Apply for probate

Use this service to apply for probate (also known as a ‘grant of probate’ or a ‘grant of representation’) in England and Wales.

Probate gives you the legal right to deal with a person’s property, money and possessions (their ‘estate’) after they’ve died.

    There are different applications in Scotland and Northern Ireland.

Fill in the application online, then send the will and death certificate to the probate registry by post.

Your progress will be saved so you can leave and return to your application.

Who can use this service

You can only make an online application for probate if you have:

  • the original will of the person who died
  • a death certificate (this must be the original or an interim death certificate from the coroner)
  • completed an inheritance tax form
  • been named as an executor on the will, or an update to the will (‘codicil’)
  • spoken to all the executors who are applying for probate, and told them you’re making this application
  • the mobile number and email of every executor who’s applying for probate, so that they can confirm that the information you provide is correct

The person who died must also have regarded England or Wales as their permanent home (they were ‘domiciled’ here).

Fees

It costs £215 to apply for probate if the value of the estate is £5,000 or more. It’s free if the value is under £5,000. Extra copies of the grant of probate cost 50p each.

You’ll need to pay online by debit or credit card.
