Apply for probate

You can use this service to apply for probate (also known as a grant of probate or a grant of representation) in England and Wales.

Probate gives you the legal right to deal with a person's property, money and possessions (their ‘estate’) after they have died.

There are different applications in Scotland and Northern Ireland.

Fill in the application online, then send the will and death certificate to the probate registry by post.

Your progress will be saved so you can leave and return to your application.

Who can use this service

Before you can create an account or sign in you'll need to confirm that you have:

  • the original will of the person who died
  • the death certificate (this must be the original or a certified copy)
  • completed an inheritance tax form
  • been named as an executor on the will, or an update to the will ('codicil')
  • tried to contact all the other executors named in the will, to tell them you're applying for probate and to find out if they want to apply
  • the mobile number and email of every executor who's applying for probate

The person who died must also have regarded England or Wales as their permanent home (they were 'domiciled' here).

Fees

It costs £215 to apply for probate if the value of the estate is more than £5,000. It's free if the value is less. Extra copies of the grant of probate cost 50p each.

You can pay online by debit or credit card.
